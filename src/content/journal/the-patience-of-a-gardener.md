---
title: "The Patience of a Gardener"
date: 2026-06-10
description: "Reflecting on why speed is the enemy of consistency in both trading and life."
tags: ["philosophy", "patience"]
---

Trading is often sold as a game of speed—high-frequency, rapid execution, instant gratification. But the deeper I go, the more it feels like gardening. You prepare the soil, you plant the seeds of your system, and then you wait.

You cannot force the market to give you a setup any more than you can force a sprout to grow by pulling on it. The growth happens in the quiet moments between the trades. It's about being okay with the boredom of watching nothing happen, knowing that the structure is forming beneath the surface.

In this journal, I hope to document the seasons of this growth. Not just the green days, but the dormant winters where nothing seems to move.
