---
title: "Learning to Lose"
date: 2026-06-15
description: "The psychological shift from avoiding mistakes to accepting them as data."
tags: ["psychology", "risk"]
---

Early in my journey, a loss felt like a failure of character. It felt like I was wrong, and being wrong was unacceptable. I would spend hours analyzing a single losing trade, trying to find the one indicator I missed that would have made it a winner.

Eventually, the shift happened. I realized that a loss is just the cost of doing business. It's a data point. If my system has a 60% win rate, 40% of my trades *must* lose. Avoiding them isn't the goal—managing them is.

When I stopped trying to be right and started trying to be consistent, the noise started to fade. The market structure didn't change, but my relationship to it did.
