---
title: "Systems Over Signals"
date: 2026-06-17
description: "Why the search for the 'perfect entry' is a distraction from building a sustainable process."
tags: ["systems", "process"]
---

Everyone wants the signal. The "buy here" arrow, the crossover, the magic breakout. But signals are cheap; systems are rare.

A signal tells you what to do right now. A system tells you what to do every day, how to handle the unexpected, how to scale, and when to walk away. Building a system is much harder because it requires you to confront your own biases and limitations.

I'm moving away from hunting for the perfect entry and toward refining a process that I can execute with my eyes closed. It's about building a machine that works while I sleep, not a hustle that requires my constant anxiety.
